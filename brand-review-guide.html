Part 1
Brand Strategy
Mission
Why We Exist

DocketRadar exists to eliminate the fragmented, error-prone process of manually monitoring Florida court dockets — giving legal professionals a single, reliable command center so they never miss a filing, hearing, or deadline again.

We believe paralegals and attorneys shouldn't spend their mornings clicking through county clerk websites. That time belongs to their clients.
Vision
Where We're Going

To become the indispensable docket intelligence layer for every Florida legal professional — and ultimately, every state court practitioner in the country — by embedding smart, automated case monitoring directly into the workflows they already use.

Values
What We Stand For
ValueWhat It Means
VigilanceCourt dockets don't wait. We check every 15 minutes. If a docket changed and we didn't catch it, that's a bug we fix immediately.
Privacy by DesignZero user data collection. No analytics, no tracking, no server-side storage. Your case data stays on your machine. This is architecture, not a feature toggle.
Practitioner-FirstBuilt for the people doing the daily work — paralegals managing 100+ cases, solo attorneys juggling every role. Every design decision starts with "Does this save time right now?"
Seamless IntegrationWe don't ask users to change. Sidebar on the court pages they already visit, syncs with Clio, exports in formats they already use.
ClarityLegal work is complex enough. Clean visual hierarchy, clear change indicators, unified interface across 8 counties, zero learning curve.

Elevator Pitch
30-Second Version
If you practice law in Florida, you probably start every morning checking three, four, maybe eight different county clerk websites. DocketRadar is a Chrome extension that puts all of that into a single sidebar, right on the court portal pages you already use. It monitors your cases across eight counties, checks for changes every fifteen minutes, sends you a notification when something moves, and syncs everything directly into Clio. Your data never leaves your machine. It turns an hour of manual checking into a two-minute scan.
