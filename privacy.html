Privacy

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

DocketRadar is built on a simple principle: your data is yours. We collect zero user data. No analytics, no tracking pixels, no server-side storage of your case information. This policy explains exactly what DocketRadar does and does not do with your information.

What We Collect

Nothing. DocketRadar does not collect, transmit, or store any user data on external servers. There is no account system for core functionality, no usage analytics, no error reporting that phones home, and no telemetry of any kind. All data generated by DocketRadar stays entirely within your browser's local storage on your own machine.

Chrome Extension Permissions Explained

DocketRadar requests the following Chrome permissions. Each is used for a specific, necessary function:

Host Permissions (Supported Court Portals)

DocketRadar requests access to the following court portal domains to inject the sidebar overlay and read case data directly from the pages you visit:

  • appsgp.mypalmbeachclerk.com (Palm Beach County)
  • www2.miamidadeclerk.gov (Miami-Dade County)
  • www.browardclerk.org (Broward County)
  • courts.osceolaclerk.com (Osceola County)
  • courtcasesearch.stlucieclerk.gov (St. Lucie County)
  • court.indian-river.org (Indian River County)
  • apps.flaglerclerk.com (Flagler County)
  • apps.stjohnsclerk.com (St. Johns County)

No data from these pages is sent to DocketRadar servers. The extension reads page content locally in your browser to detect case changes.

Storage

Used to persist your monitored case list, change history, and preferences locally in Chrome's extension storage. This data never leaves your browser.

Alarms

Used to schedule automatic 15-minute check intervals for your monitored cases. The alarm triggers a local page check — no external service is contacted.

Notifications

Used to display Chrome desktop notifications when a docket change is detected on one of your monitored cases. Notifications are generated entirely locally.

Tabs

Used to detect when you navigate to a supported county clerk portal so the sidebar can appear automatically. DocketRadar does not read or monitor tabs on non-supported websites.

Identity (OAuth)

Used for the optional Clio, Google Calendar, and Outlook Calendar integrations. If you choose to connect any of these services, this permission enables the OAuth 2.0 authorization flow. It is never used for any other purpose, and is not required for core functionality.

Clio Integration & OAuth

If you choose to connect DocketRadar to your Clio account, the extension initiates a standard OAuth 2.0 authorization flow. You authorize DocketRadar directly with Clio, and the resulting access tokens are stored locally in your browser's extension storage. DocketRadar does not relay, proxy, or store your Clio credentials or tokens on any external server. All communication happens directly between your browser and Clio's API.

You can disconnect Clio at any time from DocketRadar's settings panel, which immediately revokes the stored tokens from your local storage.

Google Calendar & Outlook Calendar Integration

If you choose to connect DocketRadar to Google Calendar or Outlook Calendar, the extension uses OAuth 2.0 to request calendar event access only. DocketRadar cannot read your email, contacts, files, or any other account data.

When connected, DocketRadar sends hearing dates and case metadata (case number, hearing type, judge, location) directly from your browser to the Google Calendar API or Microsoft Graph API to create, update, and remove calendar events. Access tokens are stored locally in your browser's extension storage and are never transmitted to DocketRadar servers.

You can disconnect either calendar service at any time from DocketRadar's settings panel, which removes the stored tokens from your local storage.

Cookies

DocketRadar does not set, read, or use cookies of any kind. The extension uses Chrome's local extension storage API exclusively. This website (docketradar.com) does not use cookies either — no analytics cookies, no advertising cookies, no session cookies.

Third-Party Services

DocketRadar communicates with third-party services only when you explicitly opt in. The optional integrations are:

  • Clio (clio.com) — case data sync for practice management
  • Google Calendar (googleapis.com) — hearing date sync to your Google Calendar
  • Microsoft Outlook Calendar (graph.microsoft.com) — hearing date sync to your Outlook Calendar

All communication with these services happens directly between your browser and the service's API. No data is relayed through DocketRadar servers. No analytics providers, advertising networks, crash reporting services, or CDNs are used for user data.

Children's Privacy

DocketRadar is a professional tool designed for licensed legal practitioners and legal support staff. It is not directed at individuals under the age of 13 (or under the age of 16 in jurisdictions where GDPR applies). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since DocketRadar collects no personal information from anyone, this concern is moot by design.

CCPA & GDPR Compliance

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA): DocketRadar does not sell personal information. DocketRadar does not collect personal information. There is no data to access, delete, or port because we hold none. If you are a California resident and have questions, you may contact us at the address below.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): DocketRadar does not process personal data as defined under GDPR. No data is transmitted to servers located in any jurisdiction. All processing occurs locally within your browser on your own device. No data controller or data processor relationship exists because no personal data is collected, stored, or processed by DocketRadar.

Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify users through the Chrome extension's update notes. Our commitment to collecting zero user data is foundational to DocketRadar's architecture and is not subject to change.
